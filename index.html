About

Daniel Faronbi is a Jazz Pianist, Music Technology Researcher, and Composer based in New York City. Influenced by jazz greats like Phineas Newborn Jr., Bill Evans, and Bud Powell, He likes to combine intentional chordal playing with fast bepop licks. He has performed for music groups spanning a wide variety of genres and has received both jazz and classical training. He has worked as a studio manager for Studio 381 at the University of Nebraska and has published three albums. He has shared the stage with jazz heavy hitters such as Benny Golson, Bobby Sanibria, Micah Bell, and Dave Stryker. Daniel has performed at world renowned jazz venues like the Django and Nublu and maintains and active performance schedule in NYC..
